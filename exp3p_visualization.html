Exp3.P — Variance & High-Probability Bounds

The non-stochastic multi-armed bandit problem · Auer, Cesa-Bianchi, Freund, Schapire (2002)

The problem with Exp3: Importance weighting gives each arm an unbiased reward estimate — but at the cost of high variance. When arm j is rarely played, pj(t) is small, so x̂j(t) = xj(t)/pj(t) can be enormous. Summing over T rounds, the variance of GExp3 grows as T3/2 — meaning a single run can have regret as large as T3/4.

Theorem 6.3: With parameters γ = min{3/5, 2√(3K ln K / (5T))} and α = 2√(ln(KT/δ)), Exp3.P achieves with probability at least 1−δ:

Gmax − GExp3.P ≤ 4√(KT ln(KT/δ)) + 4√(5KT ln K/3) + 8 ln(KT/δ)

This holds for any assignment of rewards — not just on average.
